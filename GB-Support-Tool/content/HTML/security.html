Wallet Security & why it matters.

Risk of unsecured keys?

Having unsecured keys leaves you vulnerable to malicious attacks, leaving your fund at risk. Consider it the same as having a weak banking password that's easily decrypted.

How to Keep your Keys Safe?

There are many different methods to safely storing private keys; some more extravagant than others. The main thing to take away is to never put them online or store them undecrypted on a device that's connected to the internet.



  • Never keep private keys online, or Email, or in the cloud; as these are more susceptible to malicious attacks.

    • Hardware Wallets

  • A Hardware wallet is similar to a USB flash device, designed to secure your private keys. Using a "Hierarchical Deterministic" software these wallets generate mnemonic phrases or seed phrases when initialized.

    • Offline Flash Drive/USB

  • If you have no access to a Hardware wallet we recommend using an offline USB/Flash drive to store your keys. For extra protection, you can encrypt the files on the USB in case the device ended up in the wrong hands.

    • CryptoSteel

  • Another option is to store your keys on stainless steel using Cryptosteel, which allows you to store up to 96 characters. Click here for more information.

    • Art Pieces

  • You can use art pieces to store your public/private keys, with a QR code for your public address on the front, and your private key under a security sticker. If this interests you click here for more information.

    • With Crypto Currencies, freedom comes with great responsibility. Protecting yourself against theft, natural/personal disasters, and sometimes yourself.
